The famous Sri Balaji Temple in Shrigonda is renowned in Maharashtra for its renovation and development for various gods, goddesses, and saint traditions. Known for its ancient temples from the Satavahana, Chalukya, and Yadava periods, it has been uplifted and endowed by various chieftains from the Shivaji era to the Peshwa period. Leaders like Shinde, Holkar, Vinchurkar, etc., contributed land and resources for their maintenance and further development.
Among these, the ancient temple installed in the famous village of Shrigonda is the 'Sri Venkatesh Balaji Temple, Shrigonda'. This historic site of Tirupati Balaji in Maharashtra has been home to its sacred deity for about 250 years, catering to devotees who can't travel far. The construction of this temple was initiated by HH. Pandurang Moreshwar Kshirsagar in the Shinde administration, who allocated agricultural land and funds for its upkeep and daily rituals.
Since then, worship and daily operations have been ongoing. The responsibility has passed through eight generations of the Kshirsagar family to Late Parshuram Shankar Dixit. Shri Govind Parshuram Dixit is currently managing it, supported by family members. Under their leadership, efforts have included the renovation of the temple and its surrounding area, restoring the stone structure and preserving gardens and groves.
The only trusted descendant of this lineage, Sri Dixit, and subsequent generations have kept this tradition alive. Former Chief Pramukh of the Sri Dnyaneshwar Maharaj Sanstha Alandi, Dr. Prashant Anant Suru, undertook this commitment to revitalize his maternal ancestral home. Witnessing this, Sri Rangappa and Sri Lingeshwar Maite initiated the Shivadhanushya, contributing to the vibrant colors of the temple through Laxmi Construction, restoring it to its original stone form. Chemical processes have been used to restore broken sculptures, while damaged sculptures have been re-crafted. The sacred Deepmal has been erected, and efforts for conservation and upkeep have reached their conclusion.
At the earlier entrance, a new torana has been constructed, symbolizing the revival of the traditional architecture and once again opening its doors for devotees. In this regard, a dedication ceremony will be held on Sunday, July 21, 2024, during Guru Purnima. The inauguration of the Dalan of Sri Ramakrishna Kshirsagar will follow. Upon the completion of the mahaarti arrangements will be made for the Mahaprasad for the assembled devotees.
Following this, a Bhakti Sudha International Radio Channel Bhajan Sandhya program will be presented by Dr. Ashish Keshkar, Mrs. Bhagyashree Keshkar, and Aroha Foundation's Dr. Vikas Vaidya (Patil). Dr. Manisha Vaidya will lead this event. The festival will conclude with a Naradiya Kirtan performed by Pandit Yogeshji Kulkarni, followed by Aarti. This ancient temple's preservation is an effort that we hope all members of the community, as well as their families, will take pride in.
